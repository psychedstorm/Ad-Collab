Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 2026  |  Last Updated: March 2026

Ad Collab ("we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website at adcollab.agency or engage our services.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

We collect information that you voluntarily provide when you:

  • Submit a form on our website (audit request, contact form, or intake form)
  • Communicate with us via email, phone, or other channels
  • Engage our services as a client

This information may include:

Category Examples
Identifiers Name, email address, phone number, business name
Business Information Website URL, service area, business type, advertising budget range
Communications Messages, feedback, and responses you provide through forms or correspondence
Client Account Data Advertising account credentials, campaign performance data, analytics data, conversion tracking data (collected only during active service engagements)

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain technical information, including:

  • IP address and approximate geographic location
  • Browser type and version
  • Device type and operating system
  • Pages visited, time spent, and referral source

This information is collected through standard web technologies and is used to improve website performance and user experience.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Respond to your inquiries and audit requests
  • Provide, manage, and improve our advertising services
  • Communicate with you about your account or our services
  • Send performance reports and campaign updates
  • Analyze website usage to improve our site and offerings
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. We do not use your information for purposes unrelated to our services without your consent.

3. How We Share Your Information

We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

3.1 Third-Party Advertising Platforms

In the course of providing our services, we interact with third-party advertising platforms on your behalf, including Google Ads, Meta (Facebook/Instagram), Google Analytics, Google Merchant Center, and Meta Commerce Manager. These platforms have their own privacy policies governing their use of data. We access and manage your data on these platforms solely to deliver the services you have engaged us for.

3.2 Service Providers

We may use third-party tools to support our operations, including:

  • Form processing (to receive and route your submissions)
  • Email communication (to send responses and reports)
  • Project management (to track service delivery)
  • Cloud storage (to securely store client documents and reports)

These service providers are authorized to use your information only as necessary to provide services to us and are obligated to protect your information.

3.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.

4. Data Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, use, alteration, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Secure transmission of data via HTTPS/SSL
  • Access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel only
  • Secure password practices and two-factor authentication where available
  • Regular review of data handling practices

While we take reasonable precautions, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information.

5. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, including:

  • Website form submissions: retained for the duration of the business relationship and up to 24 months after last contact
  • Client account data: retained for the duration of the active service engagement and returned or deleted within 90 days of termination, upon request
  • Automatically collected data: retained for up to 24 months for analytics purposes

6. Your Rights Under California Law (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

6.1 Right to Know

You have the right to request that we disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the sources of that information, our purposes for collecting it, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it.

6.2 Right to Delete

You have the right to request that we delete the personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions provided by law.

6.3 Right to Correct

You have the right to request that we correct inaccurate personal information that we maintain about you.

6.4 Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising purposes. Therefore, there is no need to opt out of sale or sharing.

6.5 Right to Non-Discrimination

We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA/CPRA rights.

7. CCPA Data Processing Addendum

For clients whose advertising campaigns involve the collection or processing of California consumer data through advertising platforms, Ad Collab acts as a "service provider" as defined under the CCPA.

In this capacity, we:

  • Process personal information solely for the business purposes specified in our Service Agreement
  • Do not sell or share personal information received from or on behalf of our clients
  • Do not retain, use, or disclose personal information for any purpose other than providing the services specified in our agreement
  • Do not combine personal information received from clients with personal information collected from other sources, except as permitted by the CCPA
  • Implement reasonable security measures to protect personal information
  • Notify clients promptly in the event of a data breach involving their data
  • Cooperate with clients to fulfill consumer rights requests (right to know, right to delete, right to correct) within the timeframes required by law
  • Upon termination of services, delete or return all client personal information within 90 days, upon written request

Clients who require a formal, signed Data Processing Addendum may request one by contacting us at the email address above.
